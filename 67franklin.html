What happened to 67 Franklin St, Watertown MA?

April 17, 2014

With the spirit of the Boston Marathon around the corner and a flash back of last year's terrorist bombing, one might wonder, what happened to 67 Franklin St. in Watertown where the suspect was last found?


In my opinion, one-time incidents or news do not have a direct impact on real-estate prices. According to Zillow's home value index, Watertown's home value increased 8.8% over the past year. Last year in June, 93 Franklin St. was sold for $440,000, 10% over asking price. Last year in July, 26 Franklin St. was sold for $431,000, 3% over asking price.


Some investors raise the same question when the government shutdown for 16 days in October last year. Although this inconvenience may have caused delays on the mortgage process, no significant impact was found on the relative real-estate prices. On the same day the news reported the government shutdown, I submitted an offer on a multi-family property in Somerville which had a dozen of competing offers, among which many of them are above asking price.


Then what can directly impact the local real-estate prices one might ask? There are many factors that can affect the real-estate market. In today's world, the most significant impact is on the level of demand and supply of the neighborhoods. In highly desirable, first tier areas such as Boston, Cambridge, Brookline, Somerville, Allston, Brighton, we continue to experience bidding wars. The amount of demand, overall change in demographics of a particular area and the amount of available properties have the most impact on real-estate prices. With a busy start of April, we expect another heated spring and summer ahead in the real-estate market in Boston.


Now with that said, Boston Marathon is coming up this weekend. I dedicate this article to all the homeowners in Boston and I pass my warmest support to all the runners this year. Boston STRONG.
